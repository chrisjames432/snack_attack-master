
Game Design Questions for Snack Attack:

1. What is the core gameplay loop and how can we ensure it remains engaging over time?
2. How should we balance the appearance rate of healthy, unhealthy, and hot foods to maintain a challenging yet fair difficulty curve?
3. What specific power-ups or special effects will enhance the gameplay experience without making it too easy?
4. How can we visually distinguish between healthy, unhealthy, and hot foods to ensure players can quickly identify them?
5. What kind of feedback (visual and audio) will make catching different types of snacks satisfying for the player?
6. How should we structure the levels or stages to keep the game progression interesting?
7. What types of achievements or milestones can we include to encourage replayability?
8. How can we implement and design the leaderboard to encourage competition and player engagement?
9. What customization options can we offer players for their characters or backgrounds?
10. How can we incorporate daily challenges or events to keep players coming back regularly?
11. What narrative elements or backstory will make the game more immersive and engaging?
12. How should we design the user interface to be intuitive and informative?
13. What art style and color palette will best suit the game's theme and target audience?
14. How can we ensure the game performs well across different devices and platforms?
15. What strategies can we use to test the game's difficulty and balance before release?
16. How should we handle in-game advertisements or monetization if we decide to include them?
17. What community features (e.g., forums, social media integration) can we include to build a player community?
18. How can we gather player feedback during development to refine and improve the game?
19. What is the ideal session length for a single game, and how can we encourage multiple playthroughs?
20. How can we create a marketing plan to effectively promote the game pre and post-launch?
